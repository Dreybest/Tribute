Micheal Jackson

King of Pop


Below is about the life and career of Micheal Jackson:


  • 1958 - was born in Gary, Indiana, near Chicago, on August 29, 1958. He was the eighth of ten children in the Jackson family, a working-class African-American family living in a two-bedroom house on Jackson Street.
  • 1964 - made his professional debut in with his elder brothers Jackie, Tito, Jermaine, and Marlon as a member of the Jackson 5.
  • 1970 - "I Want You Back" became the first Jackson 5 song to reach number one the US Billboard Hot 100; it stayed there for four weeks. Three more singles with Motown—"ABC", "The Love You Save", and "I'll Be There"—also topped the chart.
  • 1971 - Jackson began his solo career in while at Motown Records, and rose to solo stardom with his fifth studio album Off the Wall (1979) during the peak of disco.
  • 1980 - By the early 1980s, Jackson became a dominant figure in popular music. His music videos, including those for "Beat It", "Billie Jean", and "Thriller" from his sixth studio album Thriller (1982), are credited with breaking racial barriers and transforming the medium into an art form and promotional tool. Their popularity helped propel Jackson and the television channel MTV into prominent highlights of 1980s pop culture.
  • 1981 - he secured the highest royalty rate in the music industry: 37 percent of wholesale album profit
  • 1982 - contributed "Someone in the Dark" to the storybook for the film E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial and Jackson's sixth album, Thriller, was released in late 1982.
  • 1983 - reunited with his brothers for Motown 25: Yesterday, Today, Forever, an NBC television special. Jackson and his brothers also partnered with PepsiCo in a $5 million promotional deal that broke records for a celebrity endorsement.
  • 1984 - President Ronald Reagan gave Jackson an award for his support of alcohol and drug abuse charities, and in recognition of his support for the Ad Council's and the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration's Drunk Driving Prevention campaign.
  • 1986 - was diagnosed with Vitiligo.
  • 1987 - disassociated himself from the Jehovah's Witnesses.
  • 1988 - purchased 2,700 acres (11 km2) of land near Santa Ynez, California, to build a new home, Neverland Ranch, at a cost of $17 million.
  • 1991 - renewed his contract with Sony for $65 million, a record-breaking deal, beating Neil Diamond's renewal contract with Columbia Records and also released his eighth album, Dangerous, co-produced with Teddy Riley.
  • 1993 - he performed "Remember the Time" at the Soul Train Music Awards in a chair, saying he twisted his ankle during dance rehearsals. He was accused of child sexual abuse by a 13-year-old boy, Jordan Chandler, and his father, Evan Chandler. Jordan said he and Jackson had engaged in acts of kissing, masturbation and oral sex
  • 1994 - Jackson settled with the Chandlers out of court for $25 million. The police never pressed criminal charges. Citing a lack of evidence without Jordan's testimony, the state closed its investigation on September 22, 1994.
  • 1995 - In late 1995, Jackson was admitted to a hospital after collapsing during rehearsals for a televised performance, caused by a stress-related panic attack.
  • 1996 - won a Grammy for Best Music Video, Short Form for "Scream" and an American Music Award for Favorite Pop/Rock Male Artist.
  • 1997 - released Blood on the Dance Floor: HIStory in the Mix Blood on the Dance Floor: History in the Mix, which contained remixes of singles from HIStory and five new songs.
  • 2002 - won his 22nd American Music Award for Artist of the Century. Later that year, an anonymous surrogate mother gave birth to his third child, Prince Michael Jackson II (nicknamed "Blanket"), who had been conceived by artificial insemination.
  • 2003 - Santa Barbara authorities charged Jackson with seven counts of child molestation and two counts of intoxicating a minor with alcoholic drinks. Jackson denied the allegations and pleaded not guilty.
  • 2005 - The People v. Jackson trial began on January 31, 2005, in Santa Maria, California, and lasted until the end of May. Jackson found the experience stressful and it affected his health. If convicted, he would have faced up to 20 years in prison. On June 13, 2005, Jackson was acquitted on all counts. After the trial, he became reclusive and moved to Bahrain as a guest of Sheikh Abdullah. Jermaine Jackson later said the family had planned to send Michael there had he been convicted.
  • 2006 - agreed to use a piece of his ATV catalog stake as collateral against his $270 million worth of loans to Bank of America. The catalog was worth about $1 billion at the time.
  • 2007 - Jackson and Sony bought another music publishing company, Famous Music LLC, formerly owned by Viacom. This deal gave him the rights to songs by Eminem and Beck, among others.
  • 2009 - less than three weeks before the first show was due to begin in London, with all concerts sold out, Jackson died from a cardiac arrest.
